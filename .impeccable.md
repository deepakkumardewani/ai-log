## Design Context

### Users
Developers who use Claude Code heavily as their primary AI coding assistant. They open cclog occasionally — after a work session, when researching past decisions, or to share context. The interface is a personal archive browser, not a dashboard they stare at all day. Context: typically desktop, quiet focus mode, often late in a work session.

### Brand Personality
Focused, minimal, sharp. Every element earns its place. No decoration for its own sake. The warm terracotta palette softens what could otherwise feel cold and clinical.

### Emotional Goal
"I have a clear view of everything." Orientation at a glance — at first sight, the developer knows what they've been working on, how much, and when. Scanning should feel fast and comfortable, not overwhelming.

### Aesthetic Direction
Warm dark neutral base with a terracotta accent. Geist + JetBrains Mono + Space Grotesk type stack. Inspired by refined developer tools (Linear, Raycast) but warmer and more personal. Not a cold analytics dashboard — more like a well-organized personal archive.

**Anti-patterns to avoid:**
- Generic card grids (icon + heading + text repeated endlessly, same-sized)
- Dense spreadsheet tables with no breathing room
- Over-animated interactions — keep it snappy, transitions under 150ms
- Heavy chrome: no sidebars, no top navbars, content-first

### Design Principles
1. **Orientation over decoration** — layout should immediately communicate scope (how many projects, when last active) without requiring reading
2. **Hierarchy through space, not noise** — use generous whitespace between sections and tight grouping within; avoid borders and backgrounds as decorative crutches
3. **Data clarity** — numbers and dates are the primary content; make them readable at a glance, not buried in equal-weight label+value pairs
4. **Snappy interactions** — no heavy animations; state changes should feel instant
5. **Minimal chrome** — the projects are the interface; the surrounding frame should recede
